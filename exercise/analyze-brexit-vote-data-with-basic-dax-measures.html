üìö Theory & Explanation

This exercise introduces the creation of basic DAX measures. SUM aggregates values from a column. DIVIDE is a robust division function that safely handles potential division by zero. SUMX is an iterator function that evaluates an expression row by row within a specified table and then sums the resulting values. Understanding these functions is fundamental for data aggregation and analysis in Power BI.

üß© Example Use Case

Alright class, let's look at how you'd apply `SUM`, `DIVIDE`, and `SUMX` in your Power BI projects. * SUM: Use `SUM` whenever you need a straightforward total from a single column, like calculating total sales (`SUM(Sales[Amount])`) or total units (`SUM(Sales[Quantity])`) directly from your fact table. It's your basic aggregation for readily available numeric columns. * DIVIDE: This is crucial for calculating ratios and percentages, such as `Sales Margin = DIVIDE([Total Profit], [Total Sales])`. Its key advantage is safely handling division by zero, returning `BLANK` or an alternate result instead of an error, making your reports robust. * SUMX: `SUMX` is an iterator, essential when you need to perform a row-by-row calculation *before* summing. For instance, to calculate `Total Revenue = SUMX(Sales, Sales[Quantity] * Sales[UnitPrice])`, it correctly computes the revenue for each individual sale and then sums those results across the entire table or filtered context.
