üìö Theory & Explanation

DAX variables (declared using the `VAR` keyword) allow you to store the result of an expression and reuse it multiple times within a single measure or calculated column. This significantly enhances formula readability, makes debugging easier, and can sometimes improve performance by avoiding redundant calculations. Variables are evaluated once for their scope and hold their value for the remainder of that scope.

üß© Example Use Case

As a Power BI analyst, you'll frequently use `VAR` to define and store intermediate calculations, such as `Total Sales` (calculated with `SUM(Sales[Sales Amount])`) and `Total Cost` (using `SUM(Sales[Cost Amount])`), making your DAX code cleaner and more efficient. You would then leverage `DIVIDE` to safely calculate a final ratio, like `Total Sales` divided by `Total Cost` to determine a sales efficiency metric, ensuring your division gracefully handles any potential zero denominators.
