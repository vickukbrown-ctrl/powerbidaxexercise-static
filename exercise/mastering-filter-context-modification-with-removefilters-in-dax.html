üìö Theory & Explanation

The REMOVEFILTERS function, typically used as a modifier within CALCULATE, allows you to clear filters from tables or columns. When REMOVEFILTERS() is used without any arguments, it removes all filters from the entire table. When specific columns are passed as arguments (e.g., REMOVEFILTERS(Table[Column])), it clears filters only from those specified columns. This is crucial for creating measures that display values independent of the current visual's filter context, such as 'total sales' or 'average for all categories' for comparison purposes.

üß© Example Use Case

Alright, let's look at `REMOVEFILTERS` and `CALCULATE` together. Analysts frequently need to compare a filtered value against a grand total or an unfiltered base. `CALCULATE` is your primary tool for modifying filter context, and by incorporating `REMOVEFILTERS` within it, you can effectively clear existing filters for a specific part of your calculation. For instance, to calculate Percentage of Total Sales, you'd use `CALCULATE` with `REMOVEFILTERS` to get the unfiltered grand total sales, allowing you to accurately compare any specific product, region, or time period's sales against the company-wide aggregate.
