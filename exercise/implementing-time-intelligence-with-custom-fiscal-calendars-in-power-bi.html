üìö Theory & Explanation

The TOTALYTD function calculates a year-to-date expression, allowing for an optional 'year_end_date' parameter. This parameter is crucial when dealing with fiscal years that do not align with the standard calendar year (January 1st to December 31st). By specifying the fiscal year end (e.g., '3/31' for March 31st), TOTALYTD correctly resets the year-to-date calculation at the end of each fiscal period. This relies on a properly marked date table in your model.

üß© Example Use Case

The `TOTALYTD` function is essential for calculating a running total of a measure from the beginning of the current year up to the date currently in context. Analysts frequently use it to track cumulative performance metrics like Year-to-Date Sales, Year-to-Date Revenue, or Year-to-Date Expenses, which are crucial for assessing progress against annual targets or comparing current performance to previous years. It simplifies time intelligence, providing immediate insight into overall cumulative achievement within the fiscal period.
