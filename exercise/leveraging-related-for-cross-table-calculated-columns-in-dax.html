Leveraging RELATED for Cross-Table Calculated Columns in DAX

Difficulty: Intermediate

üìö Theory & Explanation

The RELATED function in DAX is used within a calculated column to retrieve a value from a column in another table that is on the 'one' side of a one-to-many relationship. It navigates the relationship automatically, provided that a relationship already exists between the tables. This allows you to enrich your current table with contextually relevant data from a dimension table, enabling more complex calculations and insights directly within the data model.

üß© Example Use Case

The `RELATED` function is invaluable for enriching your tables by retrieving a *single value* from a related dimension table on the 'one' side of an active relationship. For example, you'd use `RELATED` within a calculated column on your `Sales` table to pull in the `Product Category Name` from your `Products` table, allowing you to easily analyze or visualize sales performance directly by category without merging data.
