üìö Theory & Explanation

Conditional functions in DAX enable dynamic logic, allowing you to classify data, handle nulls, and evaluate multiple criteria within your calculations. IF provides a simple binary choice, SWITCH handles multiple conditions more efficiently than nested IFs, ISBLANK identifies missing values, and AND/OR (or their operators &&/||) combine logical tests to form complex conditions.

üß© Example Use Case

As a DAX instructor, you'll frequently combine `IF`, `SWITCH`, `AND`, `OR`, and `ISBLANK` to implement sophisticated business logic, such as classifying customers or orders. For instance, you could assign an 'Action Required' status to an order if its value exceeds a threshold `AND` it's past its shipping date, `OR` if a critical detail `ISBLANK`, otherwise marking it 'On Track'. This allows you to create dynamic, data-driven categories crucial for reporting and decision-making.
